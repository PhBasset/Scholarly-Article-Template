Introduction

Triboelectric energy harvesters typically generate high AC open-circuit voltage of several hundred volts peak, with relatively low short-circuit current of a few tens of µA peak maximum . On the other side, most applications require DC input voltages around a few volts with an associated DC current in the mA range, in order to provide a minimum average power of 100 µW, like for a Bluetooth Low Energy communication for instance. The conditioning electronics of the TENG is therefore an important subject and has recently attracted a lot of attention.   
If AC voltage is good enough for the targetted application, output power optimization is obtained by equalizing the realload impedance with the real part of the mechanical impedance of the TENG
